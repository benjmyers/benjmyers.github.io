Personal Project

Model Company: An Uncommon Look at the Common Civil War Soldier

Who was the common Civil War soldier? I researched, designed, implemented, and wrote the accompanying article for this project that explores the personal attributes, occupations, and fates of 92 soldiers. An abridged version of this piece was feature in the August 2016 issue of Civil War Times magazine.

Angular for the web application and D3 and Leaflet for data visualizations.

The Chronicle of Higher Education

Title IX Investigation Tracker

I designed and helped implement this website that tracks the Department of Education's investigations into colleges' handling of sexual assault. Users can search, filter, and group investigations by attributes like state and type of complaint. Investigation specifics like documents, details, and links are also available.

Django API and app, Canvas for data visualizations.

The Chronicle of Higher Education

The Flagship Diversity Divide

Protests at universities nation-wide during 2015 raised an important question: just how diverse are colleges? Using data from the Department of Eduction, I analyzed the diversity of faculty and students at flagship universities in all fifty states. Comparisons of student and faculty diversity with that of the region and nation as a whole show the stunning lack of diversity currently found on flagship campuses nationwide.

MySQL was used for data analysis, and visualizations are D3 and D4. Illustrator for static graphics.

The Chronicle of Philanthropy

Online Giving Trends

How do donors give throughout the year? This project gives readers a unique look at when donors give and the causes they support. Using Python to run a weekly analysis on online giving data, the text for this article is programmatically generated.

Python and MySQL automate weekly data analysis, along with GDAL and TileMill for rendering map tiles. Driveshaft populates the interactives (D3 and Leaflet) with data.
